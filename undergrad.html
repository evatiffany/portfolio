Here are some projects I have completed during my Undergraduate candidature (2016-2020) in the B.Soc.Sci in Geography offered by the Faculty of Arts and Social Sciences (FASS) in the National University of Singapore (NUS).

GIS Projects

I am interested in using maps as an effective way to analyse and convey data. Below are two of my more notable group projects completed in some of the GIS modules I enrolled in during my candidature.

Hover over each item to access the project. The project will open in a new tab.

  • GE3216 Applications of GIS and Remote Sensing: GIS Research Project on analysing the spatial distribution of Ostracod species in Australia.

  • GE3238 GIS Design and Practices: GIS Research Project on designing a GIS-compatible automated workflow that processes WIFI data connections on the NUS internal shuttle buses. The workflow product is a spatial and temporal visualisation of crowding patterns.
